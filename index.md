**THE CHAPTER · THE BETS**

## 01 THE PUCK

Skating to where the puck is means funding today's leaderboard: superconducting incumbents and their roadmaps. Where the puck is going, Chapter 2 argued, is wherever the decide-make-measure-learn cycle first closes on qubit-relevant figures of merit — because from that day, improvement compounds at machine tempo. So the forward-looking questions are venue and posture: which qubit family has a make-step today's autonomous machinery can actually grip, and what infrastructure pays off under every outcome. The two avenues below answer in turn; the corollary prices what they imply. Everything here is forecast-grade by construction — author bets with dated, falsifiable signposts, priced on the landscape above — resting on a cited evidence floor stated as it stands.

## 02 FIRST TO LOOP

**The bet: defect and materials qubits are the loop-nearest venue.** Every ingredient already exists there — separately. The decide-half already steers synthesis: supervised machine learning with Bayesian optimization has been trained across more than one hundred quantum diamond samples to choose growth conditions against a magnetometry figure of merit [arXiv 2025], and a peer-reviewed meta-analysis distilled sixty studies into a 1,692-entry database training synthesis-parameter predictors for the whole color-center (A point defect in a crystal that traps an electron spin usable as a qubit: nitrogen-vacancy (NV) and the group-IV centers (SiV, GeV, SnV) in diamond, or emitters in silicon and silicon carbide. Made by growth, ion implantation, and annealing — materials processing, not device fabrication.) family [Jiang et al. 2026]. The make-step already contains its own measurement, at single-defect grain: laser writing of individual NV centers where online fluorescence feedback triggers the stop the instant the defect forms — create, detect, terminate, near-unity yield [Chen et al. 2019] — and programmable write-and-erase of telecom-band emitters in silicon, styled by its own authors as qubit-by-design [Jhuria et al. 2024]. The measure-half runs at 4 kelvin, the paper's own words, not millikelvin: the fault-tolerant logical-qubit (A qubit encoded redundantly across several physical qubits so errors can be caught and corrected. The unit that matters for useful machines; physical-to-logical overhead is the race's real currency.) demonstration in diamond needed no dilution refrigerator [Abobeih et al. 2022] — so the cryogenic-tempo bottleneck of Chapter 2 genuinely relaxes at this venue. The fridge, in other words, is optional here — and most of the field's imagination assumes otherwise. The synthesis machinery itself is the shape autonomous labs already have — furnaces, implanters, wet benches, the robotic chemist's home turf [Burger et al. 2020].

**The counterweight, stated with equal prominence.** Reliable defect generation remains hard to predict and control by the field's own admission [arXiv 2025], and no public system has joined these legs: the ML decide-half, the feedback make-step, and the autonomous-lab machinery exist separately, nowhere as one unattended improvement cycle. That gap is exactly what makes this a forecast rather than a finding — and the landscape prices it: conditional on Chapter 2's loop bet resolving true, sixty percent that the first venue is defect and materials qubits; unconditionally, fifty-five percent that a published, independently verifiable autonomous loop iterates a defect-qubit figure of merit over three or more unattended cycles by the end of 2030. Yield loops are the near case; coherence-grade loops inherit the cruel-reward problem in full.

**The paired half-bet: the loop feeds the incumbent.** Superconducting qubits' dominant losses — two-level-system defects, junction disorder — are themselves materials problems [de Leon et al. 2021] [Mueller et al. 2019], and one materials swap, tantalum for niobium, tripled transmon coherence past 0.3 milliseconds [Place et al. 2021]. A synthesis loop that never touches a quantum processor can still hand the superconducting incumbents their next coherence jump; the landscape carries that adoption bet at thirty-five percent by the end of 2032 — the tantalum precedent took roughly two years from paper to adoption, and loop-credited adds an attribution bar history says is hard to clear.

## 03 THE VIABLE PLATFORM

**This chapter declines to pick a winner, and the refusal is load-bearing.** Move one, the thesis: build the loop that crowns whoever wins. Cryogenic probers, wafer-scale statistics engines, active-learning steerers, orchestration glue, and verification infrastructure serve any qubit family — they are platform-portable, which makes them the only investment whose payoff is outcome-independent. Move two: the portfolio, as landscape cards each carrying dated entry and exit signposts, scored in later editions — the living dossier as instrument.

**The evidence grade is itself the finding.** The deepest peer-reviewed floors right now belong to neutral atoms — ninety-six logical qubits from 448 atoms via high-rate codes with below-threshold suppression [Nature 2026], all-logical magic-state distillation [Nature 2025], on the lineage of the forty-eight-logical-qubit processor [Bluvstein et al. 2024] — and to bosonic cat qubits, whose concatenated on-chip error correction [Putterman et al. 2025] is the industrial continuation of this dossier's own Chapter 1 floor [Chapter 1, v1.0.0]. The most impressive trapped-ion numbers — ninety-four error-detected and forty-eight error-corrected logical qubits from ninety-eight ions [Quantinuum 2026] on the Helios machine [Ransford et al. 2025], and a four-nines two-qubit gate on an R&D prototype [IonQ 2025] — currently ride on preprints and company papers. The nearest machine sold as logical-qubit-native, Magne at fifty logical qubits in Copenhagen, is a vendor roadmap anchored by a hard eighty-million-euro acquisition [QuNorth 2026]. The superconducting incumbent keeps the only peer-reviewed below-threshold memory [Google 2019-2024], and Majorana stays gated on a contested signal, unchanged from Chapter 2 [Microsoft 2025 / Legg 2026]. Chapter 2's verification-contrast lesson repeats inside the allied bloc: headline counts and evidence grades are different axes, and the cards above encode both — entry and exit dates included, with a deliberate six-month slip allowance over vendor claims, because hardware roadmaps slip.

## 04 WHAT TO FUND

**One section, not a treatise, and fenced on purpose.** The striking fact this chapter's recon surfaced: the allied answer to Hefei's co-location is no longer hypothetical. The Illinois Quantum and Microelectronics Park broke ground on 30 September 2025, on the lakefront site where U.S. Steel's South Works once stood — dead steel mill to qubit park — with PsiQuantum as anchor, a DARPA proving ground on site, IBM, Diraq, Infleqtion, Quantum Machines and Pasqal as tenants, a shared industrial cryoplant in phase one, roughly seven hundred million dollars of state money and, in May 2026, 1.2 billion dollars of federal letters of intent to four park tenants [IQMP 2024-2026]. The co-location is happening. What no public document funds is the loop layer: autonomous experimentation, wafer-scale probers, robotic synthesis, active-learning stacks as first-class deliverables rather than overhead — the infrastructure Chapter 2 argued is the actual accelerant, and the verification culture it named as the allied comparative advantage. Everyone reports the park; no one prices the gap. That absence is this dossier's distinctive, falsifiable ask, priced on the landscape at thirty-five percent that it is funded as a named deliverable at the park or an equivalent allied site by the end of 2028. The scale is single-digit billions per year, quantum-specific — the park itself is a nine-billion-dollar build with about two billion of public money committed or signed in intent.

**The legislative clock is live and scoreable.** The National Quantum Initiative's authorization lapsed on 30 September 2023; the Senate reauthorization cleared committee unanimously on 14 April 2026 and the House companion was reported out on 29 April — both now await floor time, with chamber differences unresolved and the fiscal-year appropriations picture flagged on the record [Congress 2026]. The landscape prices enactment by the end of this Congress, 3 January 2027, at fifty-five percent: unanimous committees, unified industry and the outcompete-China frame argue yes; a midterm floor calendar and the prior Congress's identical bill dying of schedule argue caution. And the explicit handoff, by name: whether the United States restructures how, by whom, and at what scale it does research — the condition under Chapter 2's decade bet — is the subject of the author's separate economic-revival project, where it is treated at the depth it deserves. This dossier prices the quantum-specific ask and stops at that fence.
