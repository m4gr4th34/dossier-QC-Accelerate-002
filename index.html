Accelerating Quantum Computing Research Using AI and Robotics

Irfan Ali-Khan — Independent Researcher

Chapter 3 — where to stand before the puck arrives. Chapter 2 mapped the loop and named the race; this chapter places the bets. Not which platform wins — a question this chapter refuses on purpose — but two answerable ones: where does the closed loop plausibly close first, and what should someone build who wants to matter regardless of who wins? It ends with the one funding ask this dossier's evidence licenses, and hands the larger question to where it belongs. Every bet below carries a date a hostile referee can score. And three of its facts are not yet common knowledge: at one venue the dilution refrigerator is optional; single qubits are already written — and erased — by machines that watch themselves work; and the allied answer to Hefei is rising on the grounds of a dead steel mill, complete except for one missing layer.

THE CHAPTER · THE BETS

01THE PUCK

Skating to where the puck is means funding today's leaderboard: superconducting incumbents and their roadmaps. Where the puck is going, Chapter 2 argued, is wherever the decide-make-measure-learn cycle first closes on qubit-relevant figures of merit — because from that day, improvement compounds at machine tempo. So the forward-looking questions are venue and posture: which qubit family has a make-step today's autonomous machinery can actually grip, and what infrastructure pays off under every outcome. The two avenues below answer in turn; the corollary prices what they imply. Everything here is forecast-grade by construction — author bets with dated, falsifiable signposts, priced on the landscape above — resting on a cited evidence floor stated as it stands.

02FIRST TO LOOP

The bet: defect and materials qubits are the loop-nearest venue. Every ingredient already exists there — separately. The decide-half already steers synthesis: supervised machine learning with Bayesian optimization has been trained across more than one hundred quantum diamond samples to choose growth conditions against a magnetometry figure of merit , and a peer-reviewed meta-analysis distilled sixty studies into a 1,692-entry database training synthesis-parameter predictors for the whole family . The make-step already contains its own measurement, at single-defect grain: laser writing of individual NV centers where online fluorescence feedback triggers the stop the instant the defect forms — create, detect, terminate, near-unity yield — and programmable write-and-erase of telecom-band emitters in silicon, styled by its own authors as qubit-by-design . The measure-half runs at 4 kelvin, the paper's own words, not millikelvin: the fault-tolerant demonstration in diamond needed no dilution refrigerator — so the cryogenic-tempo bottleneck of Chapter 2 genuinely relaxes at this venue. The fridge, in other words, is optional here — and most of the field's imagination assumes otherwise. The synthesis machinery itself is the shape autonomous labs already have — furnaces, implanters, wet benches, the robotic chemist's home turf .

The counterweight, stated with equal prominence. Reliable defect generation remains hard to predict and control by the field's own admission , and no public system has joined these legs: the ML decide-half, the feedback make-step, and the autonomous-lab machinery exist separately, nowhere as one unattended improvement cycle. That gap is exactly what makes this a forecast rather than a finding — and the landscape prices it: conditional on Chapter 2's loop bet resolving true, sixty percent that the first venue is defect and materials qubits; unconditionally, fifty-five percent that a published, independently verifiable autonomous loop iterates a defect-qubit figure of merit over three or more unattended cycles by the end of 2030. Yield loops are the near case; coherence-grade loops inherit the cruel-reward problem in full.

The paired half-bet: the loop feeds the incumbent. Superconducting qubits' dominant losses — two-level-system defects, junction disorder — are themselves materials problems , and one materials swap, tantalum for niobium, tripled transmon coherence past 0.3 milliseconds . A synthesis loop that never touches a quantum processor can still hand the superconducting incumbents their next coherence jump; the landscape carries that adoption bet at thirty-five percent by the end of 2032 — the tantalum precedent took roughly two years from paper to adoption, and loop-credited adds an attribution bar history says is hard to clear.

03THE VIABLE PLATFORM

This chapter declines to pick a winner, and the refusal is load-bearing. Move one, the thesis: build the loop that crowns whoever wins. Cryogenic probers, wafer-scale statistics engines, active-learning steerers, orchestration glue, and verification infrastructure serve any qubit family — they are platform-portable, which makes them the only investment whose payoff is outcome-independent. Move two: the portfolio, as landscape cards each carrying dated entry and exit signposts, scored in later editions — the living dossier as instrument.

The evidence grade is itself the finding. The deepest peer-reviewed floors right now belong to neutral atoms — ninety-six logical qubits from 448 atoms via high-rate codes with below-threshold suppression , all-logical magic-state distillation , on the lineage of the forty-eight-logical-qubit processor — and to bosonic cat qubits, whose concatenated on-chip error correction is the industrial continuation of this dossier's own Chapter 1 floor . The most impressive trapped-ion numbers — ninety-four error-detected and forty-eight error-corrected logical qubits from ninety-eight ions on the Helios machine , and a four-nines two-qubit gate on an R&D prototype — currently ride on preprints and company papers. The nearest machine sold as logical-qubit-native, Magne at fifty logical qubits in Copenhagen, is a vendor roadmap anchored by a hard eighty-million-euro acquisition . The superconducting incumbent keeps the only peer-reviewed below-threshold memory , and Majorana stays gated on a contested signal, unchanged from Chapter 2 . Chapter 2's verification-contrast lesson repeats inside the allied bloc: headline counts and evidence grades are different axes, and the cards above encode both — entry and exit dates included, with a deliberate six-month slip allowance over vendor claims, because hardware roadmaps slip.

04WHAT TO FUND

One section, not a treatise, and fenced on purpose. The striking fact this chapter's recon surfaced: the allied answer to Hefei's co-location is no longer hypothetical. The Illinois Quantum and Microelectronics Park broke ground on 30 September 2025, on the lakefront site where U.S. Steel's South Works once stood — dead steel mill to qubit park — with PsiQuantum as anchor, a DARPA proving ground on site, IBM, Diraq, Infleqtion, Quantum Machines and Pasqal as tenants, a shared industrial cryoplant in phase one, roughly seven hundred million dollars of state money and, in May 2026, 1.2 billion dollars of federal letters of intent to four park tenants . The co-location is happening. What no public document funds is the loop layer: autonomous experimentation, wafer-scale probers, robotic synthesis, active-learning stacks as first-class deliverables rather than overhead — the infrastructure Chapter 2 argued is the actual accelerant, and the verification culture it named as the allied comparative advantage. Everyone reports the park; no one prices the gap. That absence is this dossier's distinctive, falsifiable ask, priced on the landscape at thirty-five percent that it is funded as a named deliverable at the park or an equivalent allied site by the end of 2028. The scale is single-digit billions per year, quantum-specific — the park itself is a nine-billion-dollar build with about two billion of public money committed or signed in intent.

The legislative clock is live and scoreable. The National Quantum Initiative's authorization lapsed on 30 September 2023; the Senate reauthorization cleared committee unanimously on 14 April 2026 and the House companion was reported out on 29 April — both now await floor time, with chamber differences unresolved and the fiscal-year appropriations picture flagged on the record . The landscape prices enactment by the end of this Congress, 3 January 2027, at fifty-five percent: unanimous committees, unified industry and the outcompete-China frame argue yes; a midterm floor calendar and the prior Congress's identical bill dying of schedule argue caution. And the explicit handoff, by name: whether the United States restructures how, by whom, and at what scale it does research — the condition under Chapter 2's decade bet — is the subject of the author's separate economic-revival project, where it is treated at the depth it deserves. This dossier prices the quantum-specific ask and stops at that fence.
